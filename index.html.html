Area-weighted algorithm: the map is divided into a Z×Z grid. Each zone receives candidate slots proportional to drought surface area (px × class weight × pixel area [m²]). Zones with severe drought get more candidates; zones with no drought get none. Guarantees that point distribution reflects actual drought severity.

ReAqua Kriging – Kriging Drought Mitigation ✕
ReAqua Kriging – Mitigation reach modelling

Anisotropic ordinary kriging accounting for wind direction, soil type, and reservoir volume to produce a residual drought risk map.

Reservoir Point Layer
Use the vector layer of points generated by ReAqua Scout, or any point layer with pow_km2 (area in km²) and optionally kl_suszy attributes.
Points layer:
Drought Raster (optional)
Drought classification raster (*_susza.tif from ReAqua Scout). Used for kriging monitoring station sampling. If omitted – kriging is based on reservoir points only.
Raster layer:
Wind anisotropy elongates the reservoir's influence zone downwind. Stronger wind = longer, narrower moisture plume. Method: geometric anisotropy in kriging covariance matrix (Chilès & Delfiner 2012).

Ordinary kriging with anisotropic Gaussian variogram. Monitoring stations are sampled from drought pixels (value = 0, no reservoir) and form the interpolation background. More stations = smoother result, longer computation.
Note: Higher station count = smoother output, longer processing time.
